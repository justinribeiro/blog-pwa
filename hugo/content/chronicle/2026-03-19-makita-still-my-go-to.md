I have a lot of Makita tools. Corded versions, a lot of the LXT, decades of tools that I started when my great Uncle Rich gave me a Makita cordless drill back in the 1990s. I still have that drill, though it's NiCad battery has seen better days.

One reason I've generally stuck with Makita is that when maintained they last a very long time. When I do something foolish, like breaking the chuck on my LXT hammer drill, it was easily fixed with an six dollar part.

Hence my need to fix our CXT Multi-Cutter. We may have used it to , and lets just say it's not so much designed to do that.

I have not purchased a lot of the CXT 12-Volt tools, though I like the small size and lightweight power. There are a lot of Multi-Cutter variants you can buy for less, but as with most things, when I dulled out our CXT Multi-Cutter cut fence wire in a pinch, I needed to repair and sharpen the tool. This required little more than the removal of eight Philip-head screws, pop the case and loosen the blade nut, and sharpen on my diamond blades.

Put the tool back together, made sure I had good alignment on the cutting edge, and the tool was back to it's old cutting ways for the most recent twin-related school project.

Sure, I could have bought a less expensive version—I see people talk about random brand multi-cutters and they look fine—if you don't had one, I highly recommened either way as they are a crafting and makers dream. But at least I know I can open, repair, or sharpen the Makita. Keeps me coming back to their tools.
