While clearing out the overabundance of spare parts I have, I came across a lone eight ball of unknown provenance. I don't recall buying or being given this eight ball—we have not owned a pool table since the early years of the now much-older college kid—so this made very little sense.

What seemingly made perfect sense in the dying moments of the weekend was to remember that the not-so-great blade shift handle on our nearly five-year-old Cub Cadet XT1 LT46 lawn tractor was broken. Broken is too strong word; the handle simply went missing at some point over the longer winter months.

A quick eye ball for orientation—I was feeling the infinity sign more than the eight—a clamp up and a quick pull of the drill press and we had everything we needed for a press fit.
